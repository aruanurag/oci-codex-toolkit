# Discovery Plays

Use these prompts selectively. Do not ask every question; choose the few that test the current deal hypothesis.

## Executive Buyer

- What business outcome has to change this quarter or fiscal year?
- What happens if the initiative slips?
- Which metric will make the project worth funding?
- Who else must agree that this is a priority?
- What risk would stop the initiative even if the technical solution works?

## Technical Buyer

- Which workload, application, or platform is in scope first?
- What is the current architecture and where does it hurt?
- What constraints are non-negotiable: latency, residency, security, operations, integration, or migration window?
- What would a credible technical proof need to show?
- Which incumbent platform, tooling, or operating model must OCI fit with or replace?

## Security, Risk, or Compliance

- Which policies or controls must be satisfied before evaluation can proceed?
- Are there data residency, network isolation, encryption, key management, audit, or identity requirements?
- What evidence format is most useful: architecture, control mapping, reference pattern, or workshop?
- Who signs off on security exceptions or architectural risk?

## Finance or Procurement

- Is the main concern cost reduction, budget predictability, commercial flexibility, or avoiding a renewal step-up?
- What cost baseline should the OCI estimate compare against?
- Which costs are usually missed: data transfer, support, backups, operations, licensing, migration, or staffing?
- What commercial milestone must happen before technical validation expands?

## Application or Platform Owner

- What user-facing or operational pain is driving the request?
- Which dependencies make migration or modernization hard?
- What downtime, performance, or rollback tolerance exists?
- What internal team will run the platform after deployment?
- What would make the first phase small enough to approve but meaningful enough to prove value?

## Qualification Signals

Strong signals:

- named business outcome
- compelling event or renewal deadline
- confirmed workload and owner
- economic buyer identified
- technical proof criteria agreed
- next meeting has customer owners and dates

Weak signals:

- no specific workload
- interest only in generic OCI overview
- no timeline or trigger event
- missing champion or economic buyer
- unclear incumbent pain
- customer asks for pricing before scope is defined
